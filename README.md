# The Wrath of Anubis

By Garry Francis

## Background

After a week of studies and conferences in Cairo and three days of fieldwork in the Valley of the Kings, you considered a visit to the remote village of El Rajid as a pleasant and relaxing diversion. Your former Egyptology teacher, Samuel Rahani, had told you about the village. He considered it to be an archaeological gold mine.

After befriending a local family, you stayed with them for a few days and learned much about the legends of the village. The one that intrigued you the most was told to you by a boy named Simhel.

Apparently, a young man named Sinuhe was forced to help in the construction of a fabulous pyramid for the pharaoh. Sinuhe hated the pharaoh because of the suffering he inflicted on him and his co-workers, so he decided to kill the pharaoh.

During the Festival of the Sun, Sinuhe snuck into the pharaoh's palace to kill him, but he was caught by the palace guards and thrown to the crocodiles. This act enraged Anubis, the ancient Egyptian god of mummification and the afterlife.

Anubis raised the spirit of Sinuhe and sent him to haunt the pharaoh and his descendants for generations to come. It is said that the spirit still haunts all those who live in the valley around El Rajid, descendants of the pharaoh or not.

This legend is so rooted in the history of the village that every year, on the Festival of the Sun, the villagers leave their homes to search the remains of the ancient pharaoh's palace, hoping to find the medallion that Sinuhe was wearing when he was killed. According to the legend, if the medallion is melted down and transformed into the figure of Anubis, the wrath of Anubis will be appeased and Sinuhe will have a peaceful afterlife.

The young Simhel also confided a curious detail to you. According to him, the remains of Sinuhe were not to be found in the ruins of the palace, but inside a pyramid that no one has ever found and was thought to have been consumed by the desert sands.

When you wake on the morning of the Festival of the Sun, you discover that the villagers have already left to search for the medallion of Sinuhe, leaving you alone to follow the leads that Simhel gave you.

This is a traditional text adventure that was written for The BDB Project. The game is hosted at [The Wrath of Anubis](https://bdb-project.itch.io/wrath-of-anubis).
